1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
===============================================

Decathlon Sports India Private Limited, marking its 25th anniversary of production in India, reaffirms its commitment to making sports accessible to every Indian through sustainable practices, local manufacturing excellence, and circular economy initiatives.

As we enter 2025, Decathlon India positions itself as the backbone of global production while serving as a catalyst for India's sporting ecosystem transformation. Our strategic vision encompasses becoming the most loved sports brand in India through affordability, accessibility, and technical excellence.

Mission Statement: To make sports affordable, accessible, and technical for every Indian while building a sustainable, circular business model that creates significant economic and social value.

2. MAKE IN INDIA POLICY 2025
===============================================

2.1 STRATEGIC COMMITMENT
Under the 'Make in India' policy, Decathlon commits to raising local sourcing in India to $3 billion by 2030, representing a six-fold increase from current levels of $480 million.

2.2 MANUFACTURING TARGETS
- Current Global Contribution: 8% of Decathlon's worldwide products
- Target by 2030: 15% of global products manufactured in India
- Local Sourcing for Indian Market: Increase from 70% to 90% by 2030
- Focus Categories: Footwear, fitness equipment, advanced sports textiles, cricket products, metal sports equipment, and optical products

2.3 INFRASTRUCTURE EXPANSION
Current Manufacturing Network:
- 113 manufacturing facilities
- 83 suppliers
- 7 production offices
- 1 design center
- Production spread across multiple states

2.4 JOB CREATION COMMITMENT
Target: Creation of over 300,000 direct and indirect jobs in the production ecosystem by 2030, supporting India's employment generation goals.

2.5 PRODUCT LOCALIZATION STRATEGY
Priority Categories for Localization:
- Yoga equipment (100% designed and manufactured in India)
- Cricket equipment (100% designed and manufactured in India)
- Footwear manufacturing
- Fitness equipment including electronic devices
- Nutrition products (energy bars, whey protein, multivitamins)
- Electronic equipment (wearables, massagers, fitness machines)

3. SUSTAINABILITY & ESG FRAMEWORK
===============================================

3.1 ENVIRONMENTAL COMMITMENTS
Decathlon India aligns with global sustainability targets:
- Net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050
- 42% reduction in absolute CO2 emissions by 2030
- 100% of sales from ecodesign products by 2026
- Over 60% of current products are eco-designed

3.2 CARBON REDUCTION STRATEGY
Four Main Levers of Action:
1. Decarbonise the company's footprint (primary reduction lever)
2. Optimize product offerings and design long-lasting products
3. Scale up circular business models for low carbon impact
4. Impact ecosystem through influence and advocacy

3.3 WASTE REDUCTION TARGETS
- Eliminate single-use plastics from operations
- Divert over 300,000 sports products from landfills by 2027
- Achieve significant reduction in product carbon footprint through circular initiatives

4. CIRCULAR ECONOMY INITIATIVES
===============================================

4.1 CIRCULAR BUSINESS MODEL LAUNCH
Decathlon India becomes the first sports retailer in India to embed circularity at scale, launching comprehensive circular business model in June 2025.

4.2 CORE CIRCULAR SERVICES
- Repair Services: Available in 95+ stores
- Second Life Resale: Refurbished products in 90+ stores (30-60% discounts)
- Buyback Program: Used equipment acceptance in 50+ stores
- DIY Spares: Self-maintenance support and workshops

4.3 CIRCULAR ECONOMY TARGETS
- Triple circular turnover by 2027 (200% growth from 2024)
- Current circular business: ₹35 crore
- Target by 2027: ₹100 crore
- Revenue contribution target: Significant portion of overall turnover

4.4 SECOND LIFE BAZAAR INITIATIVE
Annual campaign spanning 68 stores nationwide promoting:
- Product lifecycle extension
- Sustainable consumption practices
- DIY maintenance workshops
- Buyback and resale programs
